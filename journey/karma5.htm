karma

Karma: (ALL TOO ISKCON-ish. talk about modern use of the word) work performed for the sake of the body and its senses. Karma is simply the Sanskrit for work. The work we do to earn a living is karma. The work we put into having a good time is karma too. The work we put into eating, sleeping, watching TV, listening to music, having kids, or driving a car — when we do just about anything — that's karma.

And it's not only what we do for ourselves but also what we do for others, in relation to the body and senses. When we help out a friend, give to the poor, serve in the Army, or show our uncle how to cheat on his tax returns — again, it's all karma.

Karma may be "good" or "bad" (or, for that matter, mixed). So karma may bring good or bad results (or, again, mixed). These results are also sometimes called karma. (More precisely, they are karmic reactions.)

Sometimes the results of karma are imediate: work hard and get a good grade, overeat and get indigestion. But sometimes the results may take years — or, according to Bhagavad-gita, lifetimes. I may do something this life and get the results in the next — or ten lifetimes from now, or thousands. Karma and its results form an intricate web. (more)
