Hinduism

Hindus believe that there is one Supreme God who, because he is unlimited, has many forms, both male and female.

The most popular form of God is Lord Krishna (Krishna means “the most attractive person”). Another popular form of God is Lord Rama (“the reservoir of all pleasure”). Hindus consider the names of God to be non-different from God and chant these names, as mantras, for purification. Lord Shiva is worshipped by his devotees as the powerful universal force. He is always accompanied by his consort, Shakti, meaning “energy”.

Hinduism has many scriptures mostly written in the ancient language of Sanskrit. Some scholars claim that the Irish language originally comes from Sanskrit. The most famous Hindu scripture is Bhagavad-gita (the Song of God), which teaches that all living beings are eternal and spiritual by nature. Bhagavad-gita also teaches how to develop love for God and his creation.

Sacred ceremonies are usually led by Brahmin priests and include the offering of prayers and gifts to God accompanied by burning incense and chanting of mantras.

Hindus believe in reincarnation — that at the end of this life we will take birth again. They also believe in karma — that what we do in this life determines what will happen to us in our next life. The goal is to escape the cycle of birth, death and rebirth, and to return to the spiritual world of eternal, blissful life.

Hindus believe that all creatures are spiritual by nature so most lead a life of non-violence and will not eat meat, fish or eggs.

The Indian Community Centre congregation comprises about 250 extended families (around 800 people) mainly in Belfast. There are also two Hare Krishna Temples in Northern Ireland, one in Belfast and one on Inis Rath Island in Upper Lough Erne, with a combined congregation of 300.

Being Hindu in Northern Ireland

One of the things I like about being in Northern Ireland is that so many people are sincerely religious. As a Hindu I feel happy practicing my faith in such an environment. It inspires me.
Shelina Sunderani
