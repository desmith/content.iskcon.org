Christianity

Christianity is based on the teachings of Jesus Christ and the Old and New Testaments.

Christians believe in the resurrection of Jesus from the dead and in the existence of the Holy Trinity; God the Father, the creator of the world; God the Son, the redeemer of the world; and God the Holy Spirit.

Christians try to live their lives according to the message of unity and love proposed by Jesus in the New Testament.

Christianity is a worldwide religion with around 1,800,000,000 followers organised in many different traditions and structures. It is also the main religion in the UK in terms of numbers of followers and the length of its historical presence. It has significantly shaped the history and modern life of these islands, including their legal structures, public institutions and cultural inheritance.

There are about 600,000 Roman Catholics living in Northern Ireland — our largest Christian denomination.

Protestantism, one of the three main branches of Christianity, originated during the Reformation in the 16th century. In Northern Ireland the three numerically largest Protestant denominations are Presbyterian, Church of Ireland and Methodist, with around 337,000, 280,000 and 60,000 members respectively. As well as these three largest denominations there are many smaller ones.

The larger Christian churches have experienced a continuing decline in their active memberships and also significant population movements partly due to the civil unrest of recent decades. However, some of the smaller evangelical Protestant denominations have grown numerically during the same period.

Protestant

Being a Christian in Northern Ireland sometimes fills me with appreciation for the work of many Christians to bring hope and healing to our community. At other times I am ashamed when we ignore the true Christian spirit, which refuses to discriminate between people on grounds of race, colour or creed. Christian love transcends differences and can can transform society.
Rev. Maurice Ryan

Catholic

For me, being a Christian in Northern Ireland has mostly been a positive experience, but it would be foolish to deny the negative influences of life here, especially during the troubles. I am sometimes depressed by the tendency to equate Christian identities with particular political identities, and by the stereotypes and prejudices that this leads to. I would like to see good Christians of all denominations work together as followers of Christ and learn from the richness of the other religious traditions in Northern Ireland.
Regina Callaghan
