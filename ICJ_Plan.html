3. Distribution

Hard copy

Hard copies available at a price that covers costs plus subsidising further copies to be sent to main contacts. (£10-20 per copy?)

Main targets

  • Libraries

  • Current recipients

  • Devotees with an interest (IC, academics, leaders)

  • Contacts (religionists, academics, media, Hindus etc.)

  • Sell to individual yatras at a lower cost (e.g. £6 per copy for 20 or more, £4 per copy for 100 or more)

Soft Copy

Soft copies freely available via:

  • Website (html, pdf, Flash Paper)

  • Mailing list

    • Website, readers can opt in to receive a pdf version of the Journal or be notified when it is up on the web

    • Others can be encouraged to join the mailing list or to ask their contacts to join.

  • COM: Notifications can be sent to Free Forum and other relevant conferences)

4. Finances

Currently, to print and post 2000 copies worldwide costs approx. £4000. About £2 per copy, roughly working out to £1 printing and £1 postage). To regain that sort of circulation we would need about 400 subscribers paying £10 per copy.

The less we print the more the per unit printing cost is. The best prices were about £2 per copy (for 500 copies) with each extra copy at £0.70. With this kind of arithmetic (say £3 per copy inc postage). Each subscribed copy can subsidise two copies to be sent to our contacts).

In brief: 400 subscribers at £10 will allow us to circulate 2000 copies

100 subscribers at £10 will allow us to circulate 500 copies

We can also approach other sponsors as we do now.

6. Managing the change

Informing current recipients of the changes by mail would cost approx. £500-£1000. This would include a letter advising that the Journal is now by subscription or by soft copy.

This cost could be cut by about half by assuming that all the devotees on the list are advised through our e-mail campaign.
