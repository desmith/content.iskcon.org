ISKCON Resource List
 
 

WEBSITES:

  • Krishna.com — This “one-stop-site” for all things Krishna features informational text, online books, art galleries, downloads, links, streaming radio and video, an online gift shop, and a Live Help section with someone to answer questions in real-time via instant messenger. http://www.krishna.com

  • ISKCON.com — The official website of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness, this organizational site provides an academic context for Krishna consciousness. Included are sections on the organization’s mission, structure and historical roots; a list of worldwide centers and projects; an overview of the Vaishnava culture which ISKCON represents; and coverage of ISKCON news. http://www.iskcon.com

  • BBT.info – ISKCON’s publishing arm, the Bhaktivedanta Book Trust (BBT), is said to be the world’s largest publisher of classic and contemporary works from the Vaishnava tradition. The BBT website includes an e-library with several titles available as free downloads, an online book store, and information about its many branches and projects. http://www.bbt.info

  • FFL.org — The comprehensive website for ISKCON affiliate Food for Life (FFL) shares the latest news about the organization’s work around the world, spotlights specific projects, and provides facts and statistics. http://www.ffl.org

ACADEMIC JOURNALS:

  • ISKCON Communications Journal — A journal of dialogue, focusing on issues related to missionary development, communication, administration, social development and education which affect ISKCON. Available by subscription from ICJ, 63, Divinity Road, Oxford, OX4 1LH, UK. Back issues are available online at http://iskcon.com/icj/

  • Journal of Vaisnava Studies — An interdisciplinary, refereed publication that is dedicated to the in-depth study of the Vaishnava traditions of India (not limited to ISKCON), from ancient times to the present. Published by Deepak Heritage Books, 10 Basil Sawyer Drive, Hampton, Virginia 23666.

MAGAZINE:

  • Back to Godhead — Begun by Srila Prabhupada in 1944, Back to Godhead is the magazine of the Hare Krishna movement. This bi-monthly color publication covers all aspects of the philosophy and practice of Krishna consciousness. Available by subscription from Back to Godhead, P.O. Box 430, Alachua, FL 32616 or online at http://btg.krishna.com/
