About the Contributors  

Kripamoya Dasa joined ISKCON in 1974, and is an initiated disciple of Srila Prabhupada. He spent twelve years on sankirtan, ten in England and two in Africa. He has acted as the UK Director of Congregational Development for the last eight years. His is married to Guru Carana Padma Devi Dasi and they have two children, Janavi and Tulasi.
(Back to Article)

Anuttama Dasa joined ISKCON in 1975.  He was initiated by Srila Prabhupada in 1976 and served as Sankirtan leader in Denver, Colorado until 1983.  From 1983-86 he was temple president in Boulder and subsequently became the Denver temple president.  Since 1993 Anuttama has served as Director of Communications for ISKCON in North America.  He is married to Rukmini Devi Dasi and has one son.
(Back to Article)

Dr. Julius Lipner has been lecturing in Indian religions and the comparative study of religion in the Divinity Faculty at the University of Cambridge since 1975. Of part-Indian extraction, he was born and brought up in India and regularly returns there to meet friends and family and undertake research. His writings include a book on Ramanuja (The Face of Truth, Macmillan, 1986), Hindu Ethics (SUNY, 1989), Hindus: Their Religious Beliefs and Practices (Routledge, 1994) and numerous articles published in various scholarly journals.. He is married to Anindita and they have two children, Tanya (22), and Julius (19).
(Back to Article)

Prof. Larry Shinn is President of Berea College, Kentucky.  Previous to this appointment he was Vice-President of Academic Affairs, Dean of Humanities and Head of the Religious Studies Department at Bucknell University, USA. Prof. Shinn has studied ISKCON in America for more than ten years and, among his other writings, published, The Dark Lord, a study of the Hare Krsnas and the cult controversy.  Prof. Shinn is also a Methodist Minister.
(Back to Article)

Daya Devi Dasi joined ISKCON in 1985. She started a small Nama Hatta centre in Wiesbaden in 1986, which has since developed into both a temple and congregational community. She is presently engaged in temple management, congregational development, lecturing at seminars and running cookery courses and school programmes.   She is married to Parmatma Dasa.
(Back to Article)

Ravindra Svarupa Dasa (Dr. William Deadwyler) was initiate`d by Srila Prabhupada in 1971. He has been a member of ISKCON's Governing Body Commission since 1987. He is also currently President of ISKCON Philadelphia and an initiating guru.  He holds an M.A. and Ph.D. in Religion from Temple University, and a B.A. in Philosophy from the University of Pennsylvania.
(Back to Article)

Sefton Davies is an independent consultant and trainer specialising in management in education.  He is an associate tutor at the University of Bristol and the Further Education Staff College, where his clients include the University of Wales, Harvard University, Research Triangle Institute and a number of local authorities and colleges of further education throughout the UK.  He also does extensive work overseas, primarily in third world countries such as Pakistan, Egypt, Zimbabwe, Uganda and Malaysia.  He is married to Ann and has three children, including his daughter Kesava Devi Dasi.
(Back to Article)

Krishna Dharma Dasa joined ISKCON in 1979.  He is a member of the ISKCON UK National Management Council and president of the Manchester temple.  He is married to Cintamani Devi Dasi and has two children, Madhava and Radhika.
(Back to Article)

Akhandadhi Dasa joined ISKCON in 1975 and was initiated by Srila Prabhupada.  He was Life Membership Director for the UK from 1977 to 1985 and is also a former temple president of Bhaktivedanta Manor, ISKCON's UK headquarters.  He is a member of the Central Religious Advisory Committee to the BBC and Independent Television and Radio and a trustee of the Interfaith Network UK. Akhandadhi is married to Ratnavati Devi Dasi and has two children, Sairandhari and Yudhistira.
